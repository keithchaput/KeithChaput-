Start Local

The Strategic Value of Local Races

Local public offices—such as City Council, School Board, County Commission, and State Legislature—possess substantial authority over education, public safety, housing, health, and budgetary priorities. These races tend to have lower financial thresholds, smaller electorates, and offer candidates the opportunity for direct, community-based impact. In off-cycle election years, voter turnout may fall below 20%, making strategic outreach exponentially more effective.

How to Run for Congress

Federal Eligibility Requirements

  • House of Representatives: Minimum age 25; U.S. citizenship for at least 7 years; residency in the state of candidacy
  • Senate: Minimum age 30; U.S. citizenship for at least 9 years; residency in the state

Procedural Steps to Launch a Campaign:

  1. File a Statement of Candidacy (FEC Form 2)
  2. Register your campaign committee (FEC Form 1)
  3. Open a separate campaign bank account
  4. Obtain an EIN (Employer Identification Number) from the IRS
